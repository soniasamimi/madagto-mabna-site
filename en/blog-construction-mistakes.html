Costly Construction Mistakes: How to Avoid Failure in Your Building Project

Construction is often one of the biggest investments a client will ever make. Unfortunately, many projects run into unexpected challenges, budget overruns, and frustrating delays. Our 27 years of experience at Madagto Mabna shows that these issues are rarely due to "bad luck," but rather a few "strategic mistakes" made at the beginning. In this article, we highlight the most common errors and how to avoid them.

Rushing Execution Without Complete Design

The biggest mistake is entering construction without fully detailed Phase 2 drawings. When designs are incomplete, execution teams must halt work to figure things out. These halts lead to wasted time, rework costs, and lower quality.


Solution: Before excavation, ensure that architectural, structural, and MEP designs are perfectly coordinated.

Choosing Contractors Based Solely on "Lowest Bid"

Clients often assume the lowest bidder is the most economical choice. In reality, low-priced contractors often compromise on quality or use change orders to inflate the final cost beyond standard agreements.


Solution: Seek financial transparency, not just a low price. Management Contracting models allow you to manage actual costs transparently.

Ignoring Soil Testing and Geotechnical Studies

Sometimes, clients skip soil studies to "save money." This is like building a valuable asset on ground you don't understand. Structural settlement, cracks, and excavation instability impose costs far higher than the price of a proper soil test.

Micromanaging Technical Details

The client's role is to be the project owner and final decision-maker, not the technical executor. Unprofessional interference in structural details or constant, uncoordinated plan changes can destroy the project's technical integrity.


Solution: Maintain your role as the investor and high-level overseer, and let the engineering team manage the technical complexities.

Emotional Material Purchasing

Buying materials in bulk without a project schedule leads to waste, theft, and damage.


Solution: Procurement should be strictly aligned with the project schedule to prevent capital from being tied up in idle inventory.

Neglecting Insurance and Legal Responsibilities

Many clients overlook legal and insurance obligations. Workplace accidents can bring an entire project to a standstill.


Solution: Before site mobilization, ensure all required permits and liability/accident insurance policies are in place.

Conclusion

Construction is a complex engineering process. Avoiding these mistakes requires a systematic and coordinated approach. If you want to complete your project in Shiraz with peace of mind, without legal friction, and with the highest technical quality, utilize the expertise of an experienced engineering firm from the start.
